Technical Philosophy

How we approach ML problems:

  • Start with the Business Problem We don't lead with "let's use transformers" or "we should try reinforcement learning." We start with: what are you trying to achieve? What decision are you trying to make better? What process are you trying to automate?
  • Understand the Data First Before building any model, we dive deep into your data: its quality, its biases, its limitations. Most ML failures happen because of data problems, not algorithm problems.
  • Right-Sized Solutions We don't over-engineer. A well-designed Random Forest often beats a poorly implemented neural network. Use the simplest model that solves the problem effectively.
  • Build for Maintainability ML systems need to be retrained, monitored, and updated. We design systems that your team can actually maintain and improve.
  • Measure Everything Clear metrics, rigorous evaluation, and honest reporting of limitations. If a model doesn't meet the bar, we say so.
